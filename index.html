A Brief History of Chicken and Waffles

Before delving into the perfect harmony of flavors, it’s essential to understand the origins of chicken and waffles. The history of this dish is as fascinating as the meal itself. While the pairing might seem modern, its roots stretch back centuries. Some argue that the combination originated in the southern United States during the time of slavery, where slaves would use leftover fried chicken served alongside breakfast waffles. Others trace its origins to Pennsylvania Dutch country, where savory meat dishes were commonly served with waffles.

However, the dish gained significant popularity in the 1930s in Harlem, New York, when a late-night restaurant called Wells' Supper Club began serving chicken and waffles to jazz musicians who wanted a combination of dinner and breakfast after their performances. Since then, the dish has spread far and wide, gracing the tables of both fancy bistros and affordable waffle restaurants alike.
